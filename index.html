About

Background & Education

I am a Ph.D. researcher in the Haque Research Group at Pennsylvania State University, working at the intersection of materials science, semiconductor device physics, and defect engineering. My central innovation—Electron Wind Force (EWF) annealing—leverages high-density microsecond current pulses to migrate and annihilate lattice defects at room temperature, requiring no thermal input and completing in under 60 seconds.

My research has been validated across wide-bandgap semiconductors (GaN HEMTs, SiC MOSFETs, Zener diodes, AlN diodes) and structural alloys (FeCrAl, ZrTi), with direct mechanistic evidence from in-situ TEM electropulsing. I work with advanced characterization tools including SEM/TEM/FIB, EBSD, XRD, Raman spectroscopy, and full electrical characterization suites.

Alongside research, I have extensive teaching experience at three institutions across three countries—as a lecturer, course instructor, and graduate teaching assistant—delivering coursework to classes of up to 300 students.
